The rise of AI agents has introduced a new frontier of security challenges, from prompt injection and data exfiltration to supply chain vulnerabilities in models and frameworks. As developers, we're tasked with building these intelligent systems securely, often navigating a complex landscape of tools and methodologies. This article cuts through the hype to provide an honest, practical comparison between two distinct philosophies for securing AI agents in 2026: the integrated platform approach represented by Snyk Evo Agentic Development Security, and the modular, developer-centric stack we're calling Salt Code.



> **Try Snyk →** [Snyk](https://snyk.io) — Free tier for individuals; paid team and business plans



### TL;DR Verdict Box

*   **Snyk Evo Agentic Development Security:** An integrated, platform-centric solution leveraging Snyk's extensive scanning capabilities, tailored for comprehensive security across the AI agent development lifecycle, from dependencies to generated code and infrastructure. Ideal for organizations seeking a unified security dashboard and streamlined compliance.
*   **Salt Code:** A flexible, developer-driven approach that combines best-of-breed open-source tools and AI coding assistants to embed security directly into the AI agent development process, emphasizing customizability, rapid iteration, and deep integration into the developer's workflow.
